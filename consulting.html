Phase 01

Analyze

In the first step, we capture the current state of your process landscape. This goes beyond documented workflows to include the lived reality — including informal workarounds and shadow processes.

Approach

  • Structured interviews with process stakeholders
  • Observation of actual workflows
  • Documentation as process models (BPMN)
  • Identification of bottlenecks and media breaks

Outcome

Complete process map with prioritized optimization opportunities and a recommendation for next steps.

Phase 02

Standardize

Before technology is deployed, workflows need to be consistent. In this phase, variations are reduced and best practices are defined as binding standards.

Approach

  • Comparison of process variants across departments/locations
  • Definition of target processes with business units
  • Harmonization of terminology and templates
  • Creation of process guidelines

Outcome

Documented target processes that serve as the foundation for subsequent digitalization.

Phase 03

Normalize

Data is the foundation of any digitalization effort. In this phase, data structures are cleaned up, redundancies eliminated, and unified interfaces established.

Approach

  • Inventory of existing data sources
  • Data modeling and cleansing
  • Definition of master data standards
  • Interface design for system transitions

Outcome

Consistent data model without redundancies — the technical foundation for reliable automation.

Phase 04

Digitalize

Standardized processes and cleansed data are now transformed into digital workflows. Tool selection follows requirements — not the other way around.

Approach

  • Platform and tool selection (make vs. buy)
  • Implementation of digital workflows
  • Data migration and system integration
  • Training and change management

Outcome

Fully digital processes on a suitable platform, with trained users.

Phase 05

Automate

Recurring, rule-based tasks are automated. The goal: free employees from routine work and eliminate error sources.

Approach

  • Identification of automation candidates (ROI assessment)
  • Building workflows with Power Automate or Logic Apps
  • Cross-system integration via APIs
  • Monitoring, alerting, and error handling

Outcome

Reliably running automations with transparent monitoring and documented escalation paths.

Phase 06

Apply AI

Where rule-based automation falls short — with unstructured data, decisions under uncertainty, or generative tasks — AI comes into play.

Approach

  • Identification of suitable AI use cases
  • Evaluation of solutions (Copilot, Azure OpenAI, Custom Models)
  • Piloting with defined success criteria
  • Rollout with prompt engineering and quality assurance

Outcome

Targeted AI deployment with measurable value — no technology for its own sake.

Reference Project

Order Processing Digitalized

Industry: Logistics provider, 120 employees

Initial Situation

Orders came in via email and were manually entered into Excel spreadsheets. Follow-up questions went through phone calls, and customers had no visibility into order status.

Solution

  • Digital order form (Power Apps) with automatic validation
  • Workflow automation via Power Automate (confirmation, assignment, status updates)
  • Customer portal with live tracking (SharePoint + Power Pages)
  • Teams integration for internal communication

Outcome

Processing time per order reduced by 60%. Customer satisfaction measurably improved through transparency. Zero manual data entry.

Reference Project

Quality Inspection Standardized

Industry: Manufacturing company, 3 locations

Initial Situation

Each location used its own inspection protocols in different formats (paper, Excel, Access). Cross-location analysis was impossible.

Solution

  • Unified data model in Dataverse
  • Mobile inspection app (Power Apps) for all locations
  • Automatic escalation when thresholds are exceeded
  • Cross-location reporting (Power BI)

Outcome

100% digital inspection protocols. Cross-location analysis in real time. Complaint rate reduced by 35% through early detection.

Reference Project

Inventory Management Automated

Industry: Retail company, 45 employees

Initial Situation

Stock checks were done manually in the ERP system. Shortages were only noticed when customers were affected. Reorders were reactive.

Solution

  • Automatic inventory monitoring with configurable thresholds
  • Proactive notifications via Power Automate
  • AI-powered reorder suggestions based on historical data
  • Dashboard for procurement management (Power BI)

Outcome

Supply shortages reduced by 80%. Ordering process shortened from 45 minutes to 5 minutes. Warehousing costs reduced by 15% through better planning.
